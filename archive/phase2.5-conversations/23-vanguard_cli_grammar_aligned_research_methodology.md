# Grammar-Aligned Research Methodology — CLI Supplementation Path

## Basis and boundary

This document stays inside the scope of the commission. It does not reopen the grammar layer. It does not redesign the full Phase 3 operational constitution. It does not prescribe Tier 3 contracts in the abstract. It designs a research methodology concrete enough that Commander can execute one full grammar-aligned cycle now, with the outputs of that cycle serving as the operational proof from which the first minimal contracts can later be extracted.

The central claim is simple. The missing thing is not "more research." The missing thing is a governed evidence-production loop that uses the grammar as a decomposition instrument rather than as a formatting veneer. Pre-grammar research produced rich narrative exegesis, then Phase 2 reformatted that exegesis into grammar-compliant artifacts. The inversion that must now happen is this: the grammar must move upstream and become the thing that shapes what is observed, how it is labeled, how disagreement is preserved, how convergence is tested, and how scaffold-readiness is judged.

CLI supplementation changes the calculus because the context-injection problem can now be solved explicitly. The grammar layer is no longer implicit conversational residue trapped in one Grok thread or one operator's steering instincts. It can be loaded as direct working context. That means the research method can demand axis-by-axis decomposition, primitive-floor extraction, epistemic labeling, source-granular evidence, staleness anchoring, and comparison-ready output in one pass. The result should not be another dossier. It should be an evidence packet already shaped for scaffold production, disagreement analysis, and receipt emission.

The methodology below therefore treats grammar-aligned research as a five-artifact loop: commission, evidence packet, disagreement map, synthesis output, and invocation receipt. That is the smallest loop that reduces the research debt and produces the operational traffic Lane B needs.

## I. The governing architecture of the method

The method should be understood as a three-pass refinery, not a single-shot prompt.

Pass one is **decomposition**. Each agent receives the same target surface, the same modality-class axis set, the same epistemic protocol, the same output schema, and the same boundary constraints. The task is not "summarize Slack" or "analyze ChatGPT." The task is to produce an axis-bounded evidence packet: what primitives exist under each axis, what evidence supports them, what their epistemic status is, what remains thin, and what cannot yet be resolved.

Pass two is **disagreement extraction**. The agents are not asked to converge. Their outputs are compared to surface contradictions, omissions, boundary disagreements, and granularity disagreements. This is where the methodological gain lives. Three model outputs become useful only when they are forced into collision. Otherwise you merely get three prose styles describing the same fog.

Pass three is **bounded synthesis**. Commander, or whichever synthesis surface is active, does not begin from the raw web. It begins from the compared evidence packets and the disagreement map. Its job is not to invent. Its job is to construct either a scaffold-ready decomposition, a calibration delta against an existing scaffold, or a research insufficiency report that explains why scaffold production would still be premature.

This architecture matters because it preserves the distinction between observation, inference, contradiction, and synthesis. That distinction is already baked into the grammar's requirements for scaffolds and methods. A scaffold must declare axes, identify primitives, label epistemic status, connect to staleness, and pass the twelve checks. A method must carry an input contract, procedural steps, output contract, validation criteria, and scope declaration. This research method should mirror that same constitutional shape rather than improvising its own little kingdom.

## II. The research prompt architecture

The prompt should have one invariant spine across all agents. Do not let each CLI agent freestyle the ontology of the task. The whole point is that the grammar supplies the ontology.

The prompt template should open with **ratified frame**: the modality class, the surface under study, the relevant grammar documents, the closed axis set, and the output species being targeted. The agent must be told explicitly whether it is producing input toward a surface scaffold, a layer scaffold, a cross-cutting scaffold, or merely a calibration packet. Without that declaration, evidence quality drifts because the agent does not know what structural floor it is trying to reach.

Then comes **scope law**. The prompt must state what is in bounds and out of bounds. For a reasoning/chat surface, in-bounds means context-window architecture, steering surface, cognitive signature and failure modes, output scaling behavior, tool-use capabilities, inter-model handoff contract, and platform constraint surface. Out of bounds means teleological recommendation, comparative ranking, institutional role assignment, or implementation planning. This preserves descriptive purity from the start instead of requiring cleanup later.

Then comes **axis charter**. Each axis should be stated as a fixed heading with a one-line clarification of what counts as evidence under that axis. This matters because axis labels alone can be interpreted differently by different agents. "Steering surface" could mean custom instructions only, or also hidden memory and platform defaults. "Output scaling behavior" could mean verbosity patterns, reasoning-depth changes, or mode-based expansion. The prompt should disambiguate enough that the outputs are comparable.

Then comes **primitive-floor instruction**. The agent must be told not to stop at thematic prose. For each axis, it must identify structural elements at the lowest meaningful floor: distinct enough to matter downstream, but not so fine-grained that the result collapses into implementation dust. This is the grammar's granularity check made operational.

Then comes **epistemic protocol**. Every claim must be labeled as verified, inferred, or provisional. Verified means grounded in primary docs or direct observation. Inferred means supported by patterns or indirect evidence but not directly stated. Provisional means plausible but thin. The prompt should forbid unlabeled substantive claims. The point is not stylistic neatness. The point is making later synthesis able to distinguish hard substrate from interpretive speculation without rereading the whole internet.

Then comes **evidence anchoring**. Every primitive candidate should carry source references, observation date where possible, and an evidence-thinness marker. Evidence-thinness is crucial. A model that says "I don't know enough here" is doing institutional work. A model that fills the gap with swagger is producing decorative poison.

Then comes **negative obligations**. The agent must not merge axes, must not recommend what the institution should do with the surface, must not smooth contradictions between sources, and must not collapse distinct primitives into a single convenience phrase unless it explicitly argues that the distinction is not structurally meaningful.

Finally comes **output schema**. The schema should be rigid enough that three outputs can be mechanically compared. A good template is: header, scope statement, axis-by-axis decomposition, primitive table per axis, unresolved questions per axis, contradiction notes, and an end-state judgment using one of four labels: scaffold-ready, scaffold-ready-with-thin-zones, insufficient-evidence, or axis-conflict-escalation.

The invariant spine should remain constant across Commander, Adjudicator, Cartographer, and Oracle. What changes is the emphasis.

Commander should receive the most execution-oriented version: strict schema adherence, source discipline, and explicit flags for where the packet would fail scaffold validation.

Adjudicator should receive the most forensic variant: aggressively test granularity, internal consistency, missing axes, unsupported claims, and bad identifier candidates. Its job is not richness but rigor.

Cartographer should receive the homology variant: pay special attention to whether the target surface expresses primitives structurally analogous to already-known surfaces, and where it departs from them in ways that may matter for class-level convergence.

Oracle should receive the adversarial variant: find contradictions, edge-case evidence, atypical reports, failure modes, and evidence against the default interpretation. Oracle should be used to destabilize premature certainty, not to write the cleanest decomposition.

So the prompt family should be signature-adapted, but only at the level of emphasis and adversarial duty. The scope law, axis set, epistemic protocol, and output schema must stay invariant or the outputs stop being comparable.

## III. The calibration protocol

The first cycle should indeed target a surface with a rich pre-grammar scaffold or dossier lineage, and Slack is a strong candidate because it gives the widest possible delta signal. But the comparison should not be "new packet versus old scaffold" in the vague sense. It should be done through a fixed calibration matrix.

The calibration matrix should compare five things.

First, **axis coverage**. Did the grammar-aligned packet populate every declared axis? Did the pre-grammar artifact do the same in substance, even if not in structure? Missing-axis rate is the fastest test for hidden blind spots.

Second, **primitive yield by axis**. Count how many structurally distinct primitives the grammar-aligned packet identifies under each axis versus the existing scaffold or dossier-derived scaffold. The point is not that higher is always better. The point is to see where the old artifact was sparse because the research never looked there.

Third, **epistemic distribution**. What proportion of primitives are verified, inferred, or provisional in the new packet versus the existing artifact once retrospectively labeled? A pre-grammar scaffold may look complete while actually resting on a large inferred core. That is exactly the sort of fake maturity that phases love to wear like a fake mustache.

Fourth, **thin-zone topology**. Where is the evidence thin? Not just how much is thin overall, but where. Systematic thinness in one axis across multiple calibration surfaces is evidence that the methodology, the available sources, or the axis interpretation needs correction.

Fifth, **prescriptive contamination and traceability**. How much material in the pre-grammar artifact was teleological, comparative, or evaluative rather than descriptive? And how addressable are the primitives for downstream reuse? This comparison reveals whether the old artifact was structurally serviceable or merely eloquent.

These comparisons should produce a calibration judgment in one of three bands. "Mostly adequate" means axis coverage is near-complete, primitive yield differences are modest, and thin zones do not cluster pathologically. "Adequate but biased" means the old scaffold captured much of the class but systematically underpopulated specific axes or hid inference density. "Systematically blind" means multiple axes were weak, primitive yield differences are large, epistemic discipline was poor, or prescriptive content distorted the descriptive substrate.

Do not trust the methodology after one surface. One surface proves executability, not reliability. Three calibration surfaces is the minimum to trust the pattern. They should be chosen for structural diversity within the class, not just convenience. For SaaS, pick one communication-heavy surface, one structured-work surface, and one block/object-model surface. For reasoning/chat, pick one model with heavy tool integration, one with strong conversational identity, and one with more constrained surface behavior. The goal is to test whether the method survives different architectural temperaments.

## IV. The convergence methodology

The convergence question should not be framed as "how many surfaces until zero new primitives appear" in the absolute sense. In an open world, new primitives can always appear at the edges. The real question is when the convergent core has stabilized enough that additional sampling produces mainly local divergence rather than class-defining structure.

That suggests a sequential saturation method rather than a purely statistical one.

Begin with a seed sample that spans the obvious subfamilies of the modality class. Build surface scaffolds or scaffold-ready packets for that seed. Extract provisional convergent primitives from the comparison. Then add one new surface at a time using deliberate diversity sampling: pick the next surface because it is structurally likely to challenge the current core, not because it is easy.

After each addition, measure four things: new convergent primitives introduced, existing convergent primitives invalidated or split, major boundary reclassifications, and net change in class synthesis observations. What matters is not only additive novelty but reorganization. A new surface that forces you to split a previously convergent primitive is telling you more than one that merely adds an idiosyncratic flourish.

The stopping rule should therefore be based on structural stabilization across a rolling window, not raw novelty count alone. A practical rule is this: continue sampling until, across three consecutive additions, no new convergent primitives are introduced, no existing convergent primitive is split or reclassified, and all novelty is absorbed into divergent or local forms. That does not prove finality. It proves provisional class stability.

To strengthen this, compute two supporting ratios. One is the **core stability ratio**: convergent primitives unchanged divided by total convergent primitives. The other is the **novelty absorption ratio**: new observations that land in divergence or local specialization divided by all new observations. When core stability stays high and novelty absorption rises over consecutive additions, the class is stabilizing.

This is better than a naive N-consecutive-zero-new-primitives rule because it recognizes reclassification as important signal. The beast is not only "did something new appear?" but "did something I thought was universal turn out to be only provincial?" That is the real taxonomic drama.

## V. The disagreement extraction protocol

Three decompositions of the same surface should be compared through a disagreement grid rather than a prose synthesis.

The grid should be axis-first. For each axis, compare agents across four dimensions: primitive presence or absence, primitive boundary definition, epistemic status assignment, and source support. That yields a matrix where disagreement becomes visible as structure rather than vibes.

There are four disagreement types that matter.

The first is **omission disagreement**: one agent finds a primitive and another does not. This may signal source-coverage differences, model blindness, or genuine ambiguity about whether the element is structurally salient.

The second is **boundary disagreement**: agents identify the same general thing but partition it differently. One agent sees one primitive where another sees three. This is the most important disagreement type because it directly bears on primitive-floor correctness.

The third is **classification disagreement**: agents place the same primitive under different axes or assign different epistemic statuses. This often reveals axis ambiguity or unstable interpretation of the grammar.

The fourth is **confidence disagreement**: agents agree that a primitive exists but disagree on whether it is verified, inferred, or provisional. This is not noise. It is a test of evidence sufficiency.

Commander should then adjudicate disagreement using a simple hierarchy. Prefer primary-source support over secondary pattern recognition. Prefer more sharply bounded primitives over vaguer umbrellas when the evidence warrants the split. Prefer preserved ambiguity over forced resolution when the source base remains thin. And preserve unresolved disagreement explicitly when neither side can be cleanly defeated.

Model noise can be distinguished from genuine disagreement by repetition and grounding. If an anomaly appears in only one agent, lacks clear source support, and does not recur across calibration surfaces, it is likely noise. If a disagreement recurs across agents or across surfaces, or if it clusters around a particular axis, it is probably revealing a methodological or domain ambiguity worth preserving.

In other words, the synthesis agent should not ask "who is right?" first. It should ask "what kind of disagreement is this, what evidence class supports each side, and does this disagreement expose a weakness in the method, the axis definition, the source base, or the surface itself?" That is where the gold is buried.

## VI. The first-cycle operating sequence

The first real cycle should be run in a deliberately small but complete order.

Commission the target surface and species. Load the grammar layer excerpts actually needed, not the whole constitutional cathedral every time. Include the modality-class axis set, scaffold validation criteria, epistemic protocol, and output schema.

Run the decomposition pass in parallel across Adjudicator, Cartographer, and Oracle. Keep Commander out of first-pass generation when possible so Commander remains available as the bounded synthesist and validator.

Normalize the three evidence packets into the same comparison format. Then generate the disagreement grid.

Ask Commander to produce three outputs from that combined packet: a scaffold-ready decomposition if warranted, a calibration delta against the existing scaffold if a scaffold already exists, and an invocation receipt that records what ran, what evidence classes were used, what the major disagreements were, and what the resulting readiness judgment was.

That receipt is not yet the formal Tier 3 contract. It is its fossil ancestor. But fossils are how you know what sort of creature actually walked there.

Update present state with the cycle result only after the receipt exists. That matters because the institution must learn to treat research work as emitting governed state changes, not just interesting files.

## VII. The skill encoding

The eventual skill should not merely be "run research." It should encode the whole bounded operation.

Its inputs should be: target surface or class, scaffold species target, modality-class axis set, research scope statement, evidence sources or source-access plan, comparison mode flag (fresh scaffold, calibration, convergence sample, disagreement review), and operator-set constraints such as time budget or excluded source types.

Its internal stages should be explicit. Stage one loads constitutional context and validates that the axis set is declared. Stage two generates or ingests agent packets. Stage three normalizes and compares them. Stage four produces either scaffold-ready output or insufficiency report. Stage five emits the invocation receipt and any state-update recommendations.

Its outputs should be exactly typed at the file level even if the typed middle layer is not yet formalized. At minimum: `RESEARCH-COMMISSION`, `EVIDENCE-PACKET`, `DISAGREEMENT-MAP`, `CALIBRATION-DELTA` or `SCAFFOLD-DRAFT`, and `INVOCATION-RECEIPT`. That file-level discipline gives you operational repeatability now and makes later contract extraction much easier.

Its validation criteria should include at least these tests: axis completeness, epistemic-label completeness, source traceability, explicit thin-zone marking, disagreement classification present when multi-agent mode is used, and final readiness judgment declared. If the intended output is scaffold-ready, then a preflight against the twelve checks should be included even if some checks cannot yet be fully satisfied until editorial assembly.

Its failure modes should be named, not hidden. Axis ambiguity. Primitive overcompression. Source starvation. Adversarial drift into teleology. False convergence across agents. Explosion of unresolved disagreements. Context overload from dumping too much grammar into the prompt. All of these should lead to a bounded failure artifact, not a silent shrug.

What the operator provides is not artisanal steering prose. The operator provides the research brief: target, scope, species, purpose, and any live constraints. What the skill provides is the decomposition frame, the epistemic law, the comparison machinery, the output schema, and the receipt discipline.

That is the real supplementation path. The operator stops hand-carrying hidden context. The grammar carries it instead.

## VIII. Directional conclusion

The methodology should be adopted in a narrow, empirical form first. Do not try to constitutionalize it before it has produced at least three calibration cycles and one early convergence study.

The first objective is not to prove that the grammar can generate perfect research. The first objective is to prove that grammar-injected CLI research produces evidence packets more structurally useful than pre-grammar exegesis, while also emitting the comparison artifacts and receipts that operational contracts will later formalize.

If that succeeds, the institution gets three things at once. It pays down the research debt. It generates real traffic for Lane B extraction. And it learns, in bounded form, how each avatar's cognitive signature contributes to decomposition, adversarial challenge, comparison, and synthesis.

That is the right next move because it does not pretend the operational constitution already exists. It manufactures the first lawful bones from work that must be done anyway.
