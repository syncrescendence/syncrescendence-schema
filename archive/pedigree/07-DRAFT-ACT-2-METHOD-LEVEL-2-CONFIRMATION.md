## § 1. Preconditions

This act activates only after Act 1 (tranche-two acceptance) has been completed. Upon Act 1 completion, the dossier-to-scaffold conversion method registers as `tested-three-diverse` in the readiness matrix — three successful invocations across structurally diverse surfaces (Slack = messaging/integration hub, Notion = recursive block-database workspace, Jira = issue-workflow-scheme enterprise FSM). All three outputs pass twelve-check validation under independent review. This evidence satisfies the Level 1→2 validation criterion specified in METHOD-ENGINE.md § 4.2.

---

## § 2. The Advancement Decision

The operator confirms that the dossier-to-scaffold conversion method (METHOD-ENGINE.md § 3.2) advances from Level 1 (curated) to Level 2 (operational).

Level 2 means the method has been invoked successfully across multiple tasks with diverse inputs, demonstrating that it generalizes beyond its initial test case (METHOD-ENGINE.md § 4.2). The method's procedural steps, input contract, output contract, and validation criteria remain unchanged. Only the authority level and the delegation consequence change.

---

## § 3. Delegation Consequence

At Level 2, scaffold-production *execution* delegation shifts from REVIEW toward DELEGATED per AUTHORITY-LATTICE.md § 3.2. This means:

**What changes:** The agent executes scaffold production without per-output operator review. The operator receives completed batches for periodic review rather than reviewing each scaffold individually before it is considered produced.

**What does not change:** The *commission* decision remains Intermediate (REVIEW). The operator still determines what surfaces get scaffolded, when, and in what batch size. DELEGATED execution does not grant the agent authority to expand scope, commission additional surfaces, or advance to new production stages. The operator can revert execution to REVIEW at any time.

**Boundary statement:** Commission authority stays with the operator. Execution broadens. The agent produces more autonomously; the operator governs what gets produced.

---

## § 4. Readiness Matrix Consequence

**Axis 3 (Method Coverage):** Dossier-to-scaffold conversion advances from `tested-three-diverse` to `operationally-validated` (Level 2).

No other readiness-matrix axis is affected by this act.

---

## § 5. What This Act Does Not Do

- Does not commission any new production. That is Act 3.
- Does not change the authority level of any scaffold. Scaffolds remain at Level 1 (curated) until downstream artifacts are successfully synthesized from them.
- Does not affect the sensing method, compaction method, or cross-entity synthesis method — each has its own independent maturity track.
- Does not affect constitutional-decision governance. All constitutional decisions remain CENTRALIZED regardless of method maturity.

---

## § 6. Reversibility

Method advancement is reversible. If operational use reveals a deficiency — outputs that fail validation, surfaces where the method produces structurally inadequate scaffolds, or evidence that the method does not generalize as the three initial invocations suggested — the operator returns the method to Level 1 and re-imposes REVIEW governance on execution. The three tranche-one and tranche-two scaffolds are not affected by a method-level reversion; they retain their own independently earned authority levels.
