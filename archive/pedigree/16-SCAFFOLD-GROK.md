## Axis 2 — Steering Surface

**`[grok:multi-agent-ci-architecture]`** 4,000-character global instruction field plus up to four named custom agents at 4,000 characters each. Total budget: 20,000 characters across five blocks. The constraint is structural (decomposition) rather than volumetric (compression). *Epistemic: verified. Source: CI provenance report § Platform 2; pre-build memo 3. Observed: 2026-03.*

**`[grok:global-field-as-foundation]`** The global field carries the complete universal behavioral contract — identity, voice, reasoning, response scaling, anti-patterns. When no agent is selected, the global field alone governs. Named agents inherit everything in the global field and add domain-specific amplification. *Epistemic: verified. Source: CI provenance report § Platform 2 Design Decisions. Observed: 2026-03.*

**`[grok:custom-agent-modulation]`** Custom agents are modular system-prompt partitions with explicit user activation. They modulate the native council rather than replacing it. Execution topology: Custom Agent → Council (parallel) → Synthesis (Grok-led) → Output. Routing is explicit and user-controlled. *Epistemic: verified. Source: CI provenance report § Platform 2 Bridge Intelligence; Grok agent architecture report. Observed: 2026-03.*

**`[grok:steering-hierarchy]`** Agent instructions > Global instructions > In-thread directives > Native council dynamics. Higher levels override lower for domain behavior; voice, reasoning, and anti-patterns remain constant across all layers. *Epistemic: verified. Source: CI provenance report § Platform 2; Grok agent architecture report. Observed: 2026-03.*

**`[grok:agent-council-amplification]`** Named agents amplify specific council members: Veritas amplifies Harper (evidence); Prism co-amplifies Grok-captain + Lucas (characterization + divergent synthesis); Praxis amplifies Benjamin (logic + rigor). The amplification directives steer how the internal debate weights contributions per session type. *Epistemic: verified (amplification design). Source: CI provenance report § Platform 2 Design Decisions. Observed: 2026-03.*
