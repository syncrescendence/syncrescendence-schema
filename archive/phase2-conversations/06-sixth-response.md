## Part 1 — Packaging and Governance Seam Calibration

**Whether the handoff is correctly bounded.** It is not. The current HANDOFF-CURRENT.md carries a "Draft Stage A2 Commission Packet" section that is explicitly marked as non-active, but its physical presence inside the singular present-state artifact creates a structural ambiguity. Under bounded-context loading, a cold-start builder reading the handoff encounters present state, acceptance boundary, readiness deltas, governance posture, *and* a draft production commission in the same document. The "not active" annotation is a prose disclaimer, not a structural separation. A prose disclaimer inside a sovereign document is weaker than a structural boundary between documents. The draft packet should be extracted into its own artifact that the handoff references by name but does not contain. This is the same principle that governs the relationship between HANDOFF-CURRENT.md and PHASE-2-ACTIVATION.md — the handoff points to the activation record but does not embed it.

**Whether the three acts should remain distinct.** Yes, and for a sharper reason than previously stated. The three acts operate on different governance objects: tranche-two acceptance operates on *artifact authority* (do these outputs acquire institutional standing?), method Level 2 confirmation operates on *method maturity* (does the validation evidence justify the advancement?), and Stage A2 commissioning operates on *production scope* (what new work begins?). Each has different preconditions, different consequences, and different reversibility profiles. Acceptance is reversible only by withdrawing artifacts. Method advancement is reversible only by re-validating at Level 1 if a deficiency is later discovered. A commission, once executed, produces artifacts that then require their own review cycle. Collapsing them creates a single irreversible act where three separable, individually reversible decisions should exist.

**Whether any residual reason exists to defer acceptance.** No. The calibration from the prior turn holds: no residual constitutional or structural defect. Both scaffolds pass twelve-check audit. Both domain ledgers are compliant (TOOL-ECOSYSTEM after revision). No grammar-layer issue surfaced. The acceptance boundary is a governance act awaiting operator confirmation, not a quality gate with outstanding findings.

**Drift risks if present state and next-act preparation are not separated.** Two concrete risks. First, *sovereignty leakage*: if the handoff contains a draft commission, a future agent loading the handoff under bounded context may treat the commission as active scope rather than reading the disclaimer. The structural remedy is to ensure the handoff never contains material that could be mistaken for active work orders. Second, *acceptance-commission conflation*: if the operator reads acceptance, method advancement, and a ready-to-go commission in the same document, the cognitive pressure to "do all three at once" increases. Separating the commission into a draft artifact that must be separately loaded and confirmed preserves the three-act sequence as distinct decisions rather than a single ratification gesture.
