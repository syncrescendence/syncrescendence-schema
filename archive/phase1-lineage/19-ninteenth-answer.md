## Part 1: SCAFFOLD-GRAMMAR.md Ratification Review Frame

Five checks, tight and load-bearing.

**Check 1 — False uniformity across species.** The twelve checks share one schema with species-specific instantiation on three checks (2, 10, and implicitly 4). The question: do any of the remaining nine checks secretly need branching? Walking each: Check 1 (header) — uniform, all species carry the same header fields. Check 3 (granularity) — uniform in principle ("decompose to the primitive floor"), but the *meaning* of the primitive floor differs by species. In a surface scaffold, a primitive is a structural element of one entity. In a layer scaffold, a convergent primitive is a structural element shared across entities — it's at a higher level of abstraction than a surface primitive. In a cross-cutting scaffold, a primitive is a per-tier manifestation of a concern. The granularity check's test ("could this be decomposed further into structurally distinct sub-elements?") applies universally, but the *grain size* that constitutes "the floor" varies by species. Is this a hidden branching need? I think not — the check already acknowledges it requires judgment and is "the least mechanical of the twelve." The species difference in grain size is a judgment-calibration issue within a uniform check, not a structural branching need. Check 5 (epistemic labels) — uniform. Check 6 (staleness) — uniform. Check 7 (source attribution) — uniform. Check 8 (descriptive purity) — uniform, but worth noting that layer scaffold synthesis zones are the most likely place where descriptive content tips into evaluative content (the harness taxonomy's Section 8 borders on prescriptive). This is a calibration note within a uniform check, not a branching need. Check 9 (cross-references) — uniform. Check 11 (downstream traceability) — uniform. Check 12 (scope boundary) — uniform. **Risk level: clean.** No hidden branching requirement. Check 3's species-dependent grain size and Check 8's synthesis-zone risk are calibration notes, not structural branches. The three-branch design is correct.

**Check 2 — Specimen validation interpretation.** Harness-taxonomy.md fails 7 of 12 checks. The draft interprets all seven failures as pre-grammar structural gaps. Is this interpretation correct, or could any failure indicate schema miscalibration? Walking the failures: Check 1 (header) — the harness taxonomy predates the header convention; clearly a gap, not miscalibration. Check 4 (identifiers) — predates the identifier system; gap. Check 5 (epistemic labels) — predates the labeling convention; gap. Check 6 (staleness markers) — predates the temporal track connection; gap. Check 7 (source attribution) — the taxonomy attributes at document level but not per-primitive; this is the one failure worth examining. Is per-primitive attribution a reasonable requirement, or is it overcalibrated? The scaffold grammar's purpose is to connect every claim to its evidence so downstream consumers can assess reliability. Document-level attribution ("triangulated from directives for six platforms") tells you the overall source base but not which claims come from which sources. A specification author synthesizing from the scaffold needs per-primitive provenance to weight claims correctly. Per-primitive attribution is structurally justified. Check 9 (cross-references) — predates the identifier and cross-reference conventions; gap. Check 11 (downstream traceability) — depends on Check 4 passing; transitive gap. **Risk level: clean.** All seven failures are correctly attributed to pre-grammar production conditions. None indicates schema miscalibration.

**Check 3 — Production procedure vs. method engine boundary.** § 4 specifies seven structural operations. The method engine (METHOD-ENGINE.md) will later abstract reusable procedures for scaffold production. The boundary question: does § 4 stay on the structural side (what must be produced) without drifting into the procedural side (how to produce it)? Walking the operations: 4.1 (scope declaration) — structural: "declare scope and species." 4.2 (axis selection) — structural: "select from the modality class's declared axes." 4.3 (primitive identification) — this is the closest to method territory, but it specifies the completion criterion ("no primitive can be meaningfully subdivided further") rather than the analytical technique for finding primitives. 4.4 (identifier assignment) — structural: apply the naming convention. 4.5 (epistemic labeling) — structural: apply the labeling convention. 4.6 (staleness initialization) — structural: apply the temporal track connection. 4.7 (assembly/validation) — structural: run the twelve checks. **Risk level: clean.** The procedure specifies *what structural features must be present at each stage*, not *how to think about the domain*. The method engine will specify the analytical techniques (how to read a dossier, how to identify primitives, how to calibrate granularity). The scaffold grammar specifies the structural targets those techniques must hit.

**Check 4 — Dossier-to-scaffold bridge concreteness.** § 5 specifies six structural deltas: axis declaration, primitive extraction, identifier assignment, epistemic labeling, staleness connection, prescriptive content removal. The builder test: can someone holding report-slack.md and this section determine every structural operation needed to produce an enduring surface scaffold for Slack? Walking the test: the builder reads § 5 and knows they must (a) replace the exegesis's narrative organization with the SaaS modality class's declared decomposition axes from LAYER-LAW.md § 5, (b) identify primitives at structural granularity from the narrative, (c) assign `[slack:primitive-name]` identifiers, (d) label every claim, (e) add last-observed timestamps, (f) extract prescriptive content. They then follow the production procedure (§ 4) using the exegesis as source material. One gap: the builder needs to know the SaaS modality class's declared decomposition axes, and those axes are listed in LAYER-LAW.md § 5 at one level of resolution ("primitive object model, data sovereignty model, manipulation surface, auth and trust boundary, receipt and audit surface, design teleology and anti-role, failure modes, power-user ceiling, reversibility"). The bridge tells you to adopt these; it doesn't reproduce them. This is correct boundary discipline — the axes live in the layer law, not in the scaffold grammar. **Risk level: clean.** The bridge is concrete enough for an actual builder. The one external dependency (axis list from LAYER-LAW.md § 5) is correctly referenced rather than duplicated.

**Check 5 — Layer-scaffold convergent-zone identifier slug.** § 4.4 specifies that convergent primitives in layer scaffolds carry a modality-class slug rather than a surface-specific slug (e.g., `[coding-harness:persistent-instruction-file]`). This creates a new slug type not explicitly declared in NAMING-AND-ADDRESSING.md — the naming document specifies surface slugs derived from the Teleology Registry, but modality-class slugs and concern slugs (for cross-cutting scaffolds) are introduced here without a registry mapping. Is this a gap? The naming document's § 2.1 specifies surface slugs; § 2.5 specifies domain slugs. Modality-class slugs and concern slugs are new namespace entries that the scaffold grammar introduces. They follow the same format rules (lowercase-kebab-case, declared mapping, stable once assigned) but their registry home is unspecified. **Risk level: minor.** The slug formats are consistent with the naming conventions, but their registry location should be specified — either in a future naming amendment or in READINESS-MATRIX.md when it catalogs modality classes and concerns as entities. This is not a blocker for ratification; it's a downstream gap to track.

**Summary disposition:** No fatal structural flaw. The shared schema avoids false uniformity through correct species-instantiation branching (Check 1 — clean). The specimen validation correctly identifies pre-grammar gaps (Check 2 — clean). The production procedure stays structural without drifting into method territory (Check 3 — clean). The dossier-to-scaffold bridge is concrete enough for actual conversion work (Check 4 — clean). One minor gap: modality-class and concern slugs for layer/cross-cutting scaffolds lack a declared registry home (Check 5 — minor, track downstream). The document is ratifiable.
