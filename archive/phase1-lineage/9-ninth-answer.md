## 4. Architect-to-Sonnet Handoff Protocol

**Stays with Opus:** Scope-boundary decisions. Authority-level assignments. Terminological resolutions. Promotion judgments. Structural design of any kind. Drafting of canon-class documents (root, layer law, authority lattice). Drafting of method engine. Ratification of all Sonnet output.

**Delegable to Sonnet:** Bounded structural assembly where architecture is fully specified in a construction brief. Specifically: scaffold grammar (Doc 4), delta metabolism (Doc 5), identifier/heading/metadata schema portions of naming doc (Doc 7), readiness matrix structure and population (Doc 8), and the ladder diagram / track-interface table in artifact grammar (Doc 3).

**Construction brief requirements:** Every Opus → Sonnet handoff produces a brief containing: document name, artifact class, exact heading hierarchy, per-section content requirements, per-section anti-patterns (what must not appear), named source documents to load, validation checks the output must pass, and downstream artifacts the document unlocks. Brief target: under 2000 words. Structurally complete — Sonnet should not need clarifying questions.

**Ratification protocol:** Sonnet returns output. Opus checks against: (a) construction brief compliance, (b) relevant grammar checks (validation grammar for scaffolds, structural checks for specs), (c) root schema reference integrity. Pass → document enters grammar layer. Fail → specific remediation targets returned to Sonnet with the failing checks identified.

**Context isolation:** Sonnet receives the construction brief plus the specific source documents the brief names plus any already-ratified grammar documents. Sonnet does not receive full conversation history, old lineage documents, or the exploratory synthesis thread. This prevents architectural improvisation from partial context.

**Escalation:** If Sonnet encounters an architectural question during drafting — scope boundary not anticipated by the brief, terminological collision, structural judgment beyond assembly — it flags the question and returns to Opus. The escalation is a question, not a proposal.
