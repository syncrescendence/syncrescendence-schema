The grammar layer is closed. Nine documents have been ratified in dependency order, each building on the structural substrate of those before it:

The **root schema** established the constitutional frame: seven tiers, nine artifact classes, two tracks, three tracks, the bounded-context operating law, the naming law, and the promotion rules. The **delta metabolism** canonized the temporal track's rules for domain architecture, entry lifecycle, freshness computation, compaction, and sensing-directive processing. The **method engine** abstracted the production and sensing procedures into reusable invocable methods with lifecycle governance. The **readiness matrix** populated the four-axis maturity assessment, cataloged every surface and every gap, and provided the surface-to-scaffold lookup. The **authority lattice** assigned decision rights per governance family and maturity level, establishing the delegation framework that makes the institution governable by bounded-context builders.

The grammar layer is the machine that generates the rest of the corpus correctly. It is now ratified. Every subsequent artifact is produced into a known slot in a known structure, validated against a known grammar, and addressable from the root.

## § 3. The First Governed Move

Phase 2 production proceeds in two staged waves, executed concurrently:

### Wave A — Dossier-to-Scaffold Conversion

**Scope.** Convert the fourteen existing surface exegeses (report-*.md) into enduring surface scaffolds using the dossier-to-scaffold conversion method (METHOD-ENGINE.md § 3.2) validated against the twelve-check schema (SCAFFOLD-GRAMMAR.md § 3).

**Activation pattern.** Staged, not batch. Three stages:

*Stage A1 — Method proving (2–3 surfaces).* Select two or three surfaces with the richest dossier evidence (recommended: `slack`, `notion`, `jira` — these have the longest exegeses and the most structurally complex primitives). Invoke the dossier-to-scaffold conversion method for each. Operator reviews every output against the twelve-check schema. Purpose: validate the conversion method at Level 1 and begin advancing it toward Level 2 through diverse successful invocations.

*Stage A2 — Expanded conversion (5–7 surfaces).* Once the method passes its Level 1→2 validation gate (successful invocation across at least three diverse surfaces with outputs passing the twelve-check schema), expand to the next cluster of dossier-backed surfaces. The operator commissions each conversion (intermediate decision, REVIEW); execution authority shifts toward DELEGATED as the method reaches Level 2. Recommended second cluster: surfaces with moderate dossier depth (`confluence`, `linear`, `coda`, `clickup`, `airtable`).

*Stage A3 — Completion (remaining surfaces).* Convert remaining dossier-backed surfaces (`basecamp`, `discord`, `todoist`, `ticktick`, `obsidian`, `telegram`). At this stage, the method is at Level 2, execution is DELEGATED, and operator involvement is receipt verification rather than per-output review.

**Governance.** Per AUTHORITY-LATTICE.md § 3.2: scaffold production for PARTIAL modality classes is Intermediate (REVIEW) for the commission decision and Operational (REVIEW → DELEGATED as method advances) for execution.

### Wave B — Temporal-Track Activation

**Scope.** Create initial delta domains and begin populating them with observations, activating the temporal track that the grammar layer has specified but not yet operated.

**Activation pattern.** Start with three to five domains of highest decision-relevance from the recommended candidates in READINESS-MATRIX.md § 4.4. Recommended initial set: `CAPABILITIES` (high-ρ, multi-class), `HARNESS-CONFIG` (high-ρ, coding harnesses), `TOOL-ECOSYSTEM` (high-ρ, multi-class). These are the domains where information decays fastest and where stale assumptions carry the highest operational cost.

**Governance.** Domain creation is an intermediate decision (REVIEW) — the operator commissions each domain. Entry creation within established domains is operational (REVIEW until the sensing method advances to Level 2). Domain creation follows DELTA-METABOLISM.md § 2.2; entry creation follows § 3.1; freshness computation follows § 3.2 with tier-derived thresholds from § 2.3.

### Concurrent Track — Editorial Debt

The bounded editorial debt from Phase 1 is absorbed during Phase 2 production without blocking the main waves:

- **harness-taxonomy.md remediation** (7 failing grammar checks): commissioned as an intermediate decision, executed using the scaffold production method. The remediation adds headers, identifiers, epistemic labels, staleness markers, source attribution, and cross-reference integrity to the existing scaffold. This is a REVIEW-governed operation.
- **Naming-law amendments** (three deferred identifier registrations): a bounded revision to NAMING-AND-ADDRESSING.md § 5, adding compaction-entry identifiers, modality-class slugs, and concern slugs. This is a constitutional decision (grammar-layer amendment) — CENTRALIZED to the operator.
- **Project doctrine correction** (eight → nine class): an editorial amendment to the project doctrine's artifact taxonomy. CENTRALIZED.

## § 4. What Remains Grammar-Governed

During Phase 2, the grammar layer governs all production:

**Every scaffold** produced must pass the twelve-check validation schema (SCAFFOLD-GRAMMAR.md § 3). **Every delta entry** created must satisfy the entry-creation requirements (DELTA-METABOLISM.md § 3.1). **Every method invocation** must follow the method's input contract, procedural steps, and output contract (METHOD-ENGINE.md § 2). **Every promotion decision** must pass the appropriate gate (ARTIFACT-GRAMMAR.md § 4.1). **Every delegation** must respect the maturity-calibrated delegation table (AUTHORITY-LATTICE.md § 3.2). **Every identifier** must comply with the naming conventions (NAMING-AND-ADDRESSING.md § 5).

The grammar layer is not a Phase 1 artifact that Phase 2 leaves behind. It is the constitutional substrate that Phase 2 operates on. Amending the grammar layer is a constitutional decision — CENTRALIZED, deliberate, triggered by structural-flaw detection rather than by operational convenience.

## § 5. What Is Free to Proceed

Phase 2 production does not require further grammar-layer construction for:

- Surface scaffold production for any dossier-backed surface (the method, schema, and identifier conventions are ratified)
- Delta domain creation for any domain meeting the three definition criteria (DELTA-METABOLISM.md § 2.1)
- Sensing-directive processing for any established domain (the sensing method and entry format are ratified)
- Compaction for any domain exceeding its volume or cadence trigger (the compaction method and protocol are ratified)
- Readiness matrix updates on any trigger event (the schema and maintenance rules are ratified)
- Teleological manual production for any scaffolded surface (the specification class grammar is ratified; the specification-production method is an open slot, but the class grammar provides sufficient structural guidance for initial production)

Phase 2 production **does** require further grammar-layer construction or upstream specification for:

- Layer scaffold production (the cross-entity synthesis method is Level 0 — it must be validated through at least one successful invocation before broad use)
- Cross-cutting scaffold production (no method exists; the scaffold grammar's species requirements provide structural guidance but no procedural method)
- Specification production from scaffolds at scale (the method is an open slot; individual specifications can be produced using the class grammar as guidance, but a ratified method would make this repeatable)
- Typed-middle-layer contract specification (Tier 3 remains OPEN SLOT — operational proof is needed before contracts can be designed)
