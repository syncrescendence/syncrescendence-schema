## 5. Architect-to-Sonnet Handoff Protocol

**Rule 1: Architecture stays with Opus.** Any task that requires: choosing what to include or exclude, defining scope boundaries, assigning authority levels, resolving terminological collisions, making promotion decisions, or judging whether a structural design is complete. These are non-delegable.

**Rule 2: Bounded structural assembly goes to Sonnet.** Any task where: the architecture is fully specified (every section heading, every content requirement, every anti-pattern to avoid is defined in a brief), the drafting work is assembly of already-specified components into prose, and the output can be validated against the scaffold grammar's twelve checks or equivalent structural criteria. Sonnet receives a construction brief that specifies: exact section structure, what each section must contain, what it must not contain, what sources to draw from, and what validation checks the output must pass.

**Rule 3: Every Sonnet output returns to Opus for ratification before entering the grammar layer.** No Sonnet-drafted document becomes active until Opus has verified it against: the construction brief (did it follow the specification?), the relevant grammar checks (does it pass validation?), and the root schema (does it reference correctly and fit its declared slot?). Ratification is a structural check, not an editorial polish — if the document passes its checks, it enters. If it fails, it gets specific remediation targets and returns to Sonnet for revision.

**Rule 4: Sonnet never receives the full corpus as context.** Sonnet receives: the construction brief, the specific source documents the brief names, and the grammar documents that have already been ratified (if any). It does not receive the full conversation history, the old lineage documents, or the exploratory synthesis. This prevents Sonnet from improvising architecture from partial context — it can only build from the specifications it is given.

**Rule 5: Construction briefs are the interface artifact.** Every Opus → Sonnet delegation produces a construction brief as the handoff artifact. The brief is a specification for a single bounded drafting task. It contains: document name, artifact class, exact section structure (heading hierarchy), per-section content requirements, per-section anti-patterns, source documents to load, validation checks to pass, and the downstream artifacts the document unlocks. The brief is concise (target: under 2000 words) and structurally complete (Sonnet should not need to ask clarifying questions if the brief is well-written).

**Rule 6: Escalation.** If Sonnet encounters an architectural question during drafting — a scope boundary that the brief did not anticipate, a terminological collision, a structural decision that requires judgment beyond assembly — it flags the question and returns to Opus rather than resolving it independently. The escalation is a question, not a proposal. Opus resolves and updates the brief.

## 6. Purge Threshold Criteria

No lineage document is purged until all three conditions are met:

**Condition 1: Functional subsumption.** A specific grammar document or set of grammar documents now contains or enables reconstruction of every functionally load-bearing claim in the lineage document. "Functionally load-bearing" means: the claim either describes a primitive, specifies a mechanism, defines a boundary, or encodes a method that downstream artifacts depend on. Claims that are rhetorical, historical, or motivational do not count — their loss does not functionally impair the knowledge system.

**Condition 2: Provenance preservation.** The method pedigree of the lineage document — what research produced it, what prompts elicited it, what adversarial checks were applied — has been captured in the method engine or in a Level 0 inquiry-lineage archive entry. The lineage document's conclusions are consumed; its method is preserved.

**Condition 3: No active downstream dependency.** No active artifact in the grammar or content layer currently references the lineage document by name as a source. All references have been updated to point to the grammar document that subsumed the content.

When all three conditions are met for a specific lineage document, it is moved to an explicit archive location (not deleted) with a note: "subsumed by [grammar document names], [date]. Method pedigree preserved in [location]. Archived, not active." The archive is permanent but is never loaded into active sessions.

**Current assessment:** No lineage document currently meets all three conditions. Condition 1 cannot be met until the grammar documents exist. The earliest purge candidates will be the grok-monorepo-directive and grok-live-sensing transcripts (whose functional content was already absorbed into ARCHITECTURE.md and the definitive_monorepo_spec), but even these must wait until LAYER-LAW.md and the readiness matrix confirm that no residual claims were lost in the absorption.
