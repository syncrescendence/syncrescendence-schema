## I. What Can Be Canonized Now

### The Artifact Taxonomy

The eight artifact classes from the project doctrine — canon, scaffold, dossier, specification, compression, ledger, registry, method — are stable. They have been validated empirically: every document in the corpus classifies cleanly into one of these types, and the classification predicts the document's actual structural behavior (its authority level, its update cadence, its relationship to other artifacts, its compressibility). The first audit demonstrated this by classifying all project files and finding no cases where the taxonomy failed to resolve. The classification also correctly predicted which documents were overclaiming authority (the Holistic Strategic Endeavor declaring itself "canon" while containing ephemeral progress accounting) and which were underclaiming (the ontology report being classified as "dossier" when it contains some of the clearest architectural reasoning in the corpus).

The taxonomy needs one addition that the dossier layer revealed: the **teleological manual** as a distinct species within specification. It is prescriptive (like a specification) but grounded in adversarial operational evidence (like a dossier). The Perplexity manual is the prototype. This hybrid class earns its own name because it serves a function no other artifact class serves: converting primitive understanding into routing logic for the operating institution. I would formalize this either as a subclass of specification or as a ninth class. The cleaner move is probably to keep eight classes and recognize that teleological manuals are specifications whose evidential substrate is a dossier — the artifact ladder handles the relationship.

**Verdict: canonize now.** The taxonomy is stable, proven, and open-world compatible. New artifact types would need to demonstrate that they cannot be classified under the existing eight before the taxonomy would require revision.

### The Dossier Archetypes

Four archetypes: primitive exegesis, teleological manual, first-principles analytical framework, and inquiry transcript. These emerged inductively from inspecting the actual corpus rather than being imposed a priori. Each archetype has distinct generative requirements, distinct compression properties, and distinct downstream utility. The archetypes are exhaustive over the current corpus and predict what kinds of artifacts will be needed for not-yet-elucidated domains: any new surface will need a primitive exegesis; any surface that becomes an active operational organ will need a teleological manual; any domain that requires architectural framing will need a first-principles framework; and the research that produces all of these will generate inquiry transcripts as pedigree.

**Verdict: canonize now.** The archetypes are stable and open-world compatible. If a fifth archetype emerges from future research, it will be recognizable as genuinely new precisely because the existing four are well-defined.

### The Artifact Ladder

The six-level ladder — inquiry lineage → primitive exegesis → teleological manual / cross-entity taxonomy → adapter contract → registry row → canonical delta — has been validated in one domain end-to-end (the harness research lineage through ARCHITECTURE.md) and partially validated in the exocortex domain (Level 1 exegeses exist; Levels 2–5 exist for Perplexity only). The ladder works in both directions: progressive summarization (exegesis compresses to registry row) and progressive expansion (registry row unpacks to full exegesis and inquiry lineage). This bidirectional property is what makes it a genuine knowledge architecture rather than just a filing system.

The ladder needs one refinement from the current analysis: Level 2 should formally split into two parallel tracks — **teleological manual** (per-surface operational specification) and **cross-entity taxonomy** (cross-surface synthesis). Both depend on Level 1 exegeses, but they serve different functions and are produced by different methods. The teleological manual converts a single surface's primitives into routing logic; the cross-entity taxonomy extracts convergent structural patterns across surfaces. Both feed into Level 4 (adapter contracts) and Level 5 (registry rows), but through different channels.

**Verdict: canonize now.** The ladder is structurally sound and open-world compatible. Adding a new surface means populating the ladder for that surface, not redesigning the ladder.

### The Research Method

The three-stage pipeline — per-entity deep decomposition with fixed axes → cross-entity synthesis with convergence extraction → compressed taxonomy with preserved divergence — is the generative engine behind the strongest artifacts in the corpus. Its specific primitives (four-tier source discipline, progressive depth stacking, isomorphic decomposition axes, mechanism-first output grammar, falsifiability injection, naming normalization) have been extracted and can be specified as a reusable method artifact. The method is domain-agnostic: it produced the harness taxonomy in the coding domain and could produce equivalent taxonomies for exocortex surfaces, reasoning surfaces, automation layers, or any other domain where multiple entities need comparative decomposition.

**Verdict: canonize now as a method artifact.** This is the most valuable single thing the corpus has produced that is not itself content — it is the procedure for producing high-fidelity content. Formalizing it means future research cycles across any domain can invoke the method by reference rather than reinventing it.

### The Promotion Rules

The corpus contains implicit but consistent promotion logic: artifacts earn authority through evidential substrate, not through declaration. The harness taxonomy earned its authority because it was produced through a documented method from documented evidence. The Holistic Strategic Endeavor overclaimed authority because it declared itself canon without having been subjected to the same evidential discipline. The promotion rules can be formalized as:

An artifact promotes from scaffold to canon when: (a) its claims are grounded in documented evidence at each epistemic level, (b) it has survived adversarial testing or refutation cycles (as ARCHITECTURE.md's epistemic status table demonstrates), (c) it is the product of a documented method whose inquiry lineage is preserved, and (d) its scope boundaries are explicitly stated. Authority is earned through coherence, explanatory power, consistency with the corpus, and structural usefulness — the exact criteria the project doctrine already states.

**Verdict: canonize now.** These rules are already implicitly operative. Making them explicit prevents future authority inflation.

### The Naming Grammar

The project doctrine specifies: "Name artifacts by function, authority, and structural position. One term maps to one role. Collapse synonyms. Split polysemous terms." This grammar has been partially applied (the artifact taxonomy uses functional names) but not fully enforced (the corpus still contains overlapping terms like "federal stack" and "exocortex" used interchangeably). Canonizing the naming grammar means committing to a specific vocabulary where each term has exactly one structural role.

**Verdict: canonize now, but the specific vocabulary requires a reconciliation pass.** The grammar (the rules for naming) can be canonized. The dictionary (the specific terms and their assignments) requires the broad layer model to be fixed first, because term assignments depend on layer boundaries.

### The Boundary Law

"Repo ratifies, exocortex coordinates, ontology projects." This sentence has survived every document in the corpus. It appears in at least five artifacts, always in the same form, always as a governing constraint. It has earned canonical authority through convergent restatement — every analysis that touches the question of sovereignty arrives at this boundary independently.

**Verdict: canonize now.** This is the most stable claim in the entire corpus.

## II. The Broad Layer Model: Can It Be Fixed Now?

Yes, but with a specific caveat. The layer model can be fixed at the resolution of **named functional tiers with declared scope**, even though many tiers lack specification-grade content. The competing layer models in the corpus (the outline's 15 layers, the FEDERAL_STACK's 8 layers, the operational taxonomy's 6 strata) are not actually contradictory — they address different scopes at different resolutions. The reconciliation is not difficult once you recognize that:

The operational taxonomy's 6 strata (atoms → modules → instructional structures → agentic entities → platform/workflow architectures → ecosystem) describe the **configuration mereology** — how behavioral instructions compose from primitives to institutional character. This is a vertical slice through the system, not a horizontal layer model. It should be canonized as the mereological grammar for one specific domain (configuration and instruction architecture), not as the layer model for the whole institution.

The FEDERAL_STACK's 8 layers (constitutional kernel → typed middle → coding swarm → adjudication/cockpit → workflow/decomposition → exocortical action bus → observability → ontology/terminal) describe the **operational stack** — how execution flows from doctrine through typed contracts to runtime surfaces. This is closer to a genuine layer model but conflates some layers that should be separated (observability is orthogonal, not a layer; the ontology is a projection surface, not a layer above the action bus).

The outline's 15 layers are the most complete enumeration but overspecify — some of its "layers" are actually aspects of other layers (the "reversibility layer" is a property of all layers, not a layer of its own; the "human terminal layer" is a projection surface, like the ontology).

The reconciliation I'd propose — and this is a judgment, subject to your correction — is a model with **seven functional tiers and three orthogonal concerns**:

**Tier 1: Environmental physics.** What kind of system is this? What are the control variables (ρ, κ, λ, σ)? What failure envelope governs the whole institution? This tier is the analytical ground. The project management first-principles framework belongs here.

**Tier 2: Constitutional law.** What is sovereign, what can ratify, what must never become hidden authority, what promotion means, what the boundary law is. The enduring strategic pillars from the Holistic Strategic Endeavor belong here once extracted from their progress-accounting wrapper.

**Tier 3: Typed middle layer.** The contracts, envelopes, receipts, and schemas that enforce the constitutional law at the interface boundaries between all other tiers. TaskEnvelope, RoutingDecision, ArtifactReceipt, PromotionDecision, AuthBoundary, RiskClass. This tier is named but not yet specified. Its specifications depend on operational proof.

**Tier 4: Execution surfaces.** This tier contains all surfaces that do work — subdivided by modality rather than listed exhaustively. Repo-native coding harnesses. Reasoning/chat surfaces. Daemonized runtimes. Browser/operator bridges. Recon/sensing surfaces. SaaS work/knowledge/automation platforms. Cloud and backend substrates. Model-routing and memory layers. The solopreneur stack surfaces. Each modality class is a slot that can contain any number of specific surfaces; new surfaces populate slots without restructuring the tier.

**Tier 5: Coordination and event choreography.** Operator buses (Slack, Discord), automation engines (Make, Zapier), webhook/event systems, inter-agent communication, approval flows, receipt routing. This is where the institution's nervous system lives — the wiring between execution surfaces.

**Tier 6: Observability and evaluation.** Traces, metrics, logs, receipts, golden tasks, eval harnesses, adoption telemetry. Orthogonal to all execution and coordination surfaces — it inspects them all. OpenTelemetry, Phoenix, and surface-specific monitoring.

**Tier 7: Projection and terminal.** The ontology as typed projection layer. The syncrescendence.com human terminal. Dashboards, query surfaces, progressive disclosure views. This tier consumes state from all other tiers and renders it legible. It projects but does not legislate.

**Three orthogonal concerns** that apply across all tiers rather than occupying their own layers: **trust and identity boundaries** (who has what auth at every tier), **reversibility and migration** (how every tier can be rolled back or replaced), and **epistemic status** (what is verified, inferred, provisional, or aspirational at every tier).

This model is open-world: adding a new execution surface means populating a slot in Tier 4. Adding a new coordination mechanism means populating Tier 5. Adding a new observability tool means populating Tier 6. The tier structure itself does not need to change when content is added.

**Verdict: the broad layer model can be fixed now at this resolution.** The specific content of each tier — which surfaces, which contracts, which coordination mechanisms — remains incomplete and that is fine. The tier structure is stable enough to canonize because it is derived from functional distinctions that hold regardless of which specific surfaces populate each tier.
