## Pre-Draft Calibration

Five questions, tight.

**Question 1 — Four-axis sufficiency and non-overlap.** The four axes are: tier/modality-class maturity (Axis 1), scaffold coverage (Axis 2), method coverage (Axis 3), temporal-domain coverage (Axis 4). Non-overlap test: can an entity appear on exactly one axis? Tiers and modality classes appear on Axis 1 only. Scaffolds appear on Axis 2 only. Methods appear on Axis 3 only. Domains appear on Axis 4 only. No entity spans axes. Cross-axis *references* exist (a scaffold-coverage entry references its tier context, a domain entry references its tier for freshness derivation), but references are not overlap — they are the relational glue that makes the registry queryable across axes. Sufficiency test: is there an institutional maturity dimension that none of the four axes captures? One candidate: grammar-layer document completeness itself — the nine documents and their ratification status. This is arguably Axis 1 (Tier 2 constitutional law maturity) but could warrant its own tracking. The resolution: grammar-layer completeness is an Axis 1 entry under Tier 2, assessed as IN PROGRESS until all nine are ratified and MATURE thereafter. No fifth axis needed.

**Question 2 — Registry discipline vs. narrative drift.** The main drift vector: the initial population (§ 4) will tempt the drafter into writing per-tier narrative assessments rather than filling schema-governed rows. The counter: every entry must fit the declared schema from § 3. If a piece of information does not fit a schema field, it does not belong in the registry. Contextual notes that explain *why* a tier is at a given maturity level belong in the "blockers" or "next milestone" fields, not in prose paragraphs between entries. The document's rendering format (likely markdown tables for each axis) should enforce the schema structurally — a table row cannot drift into a paragraph.

**Question 3 — Scaffold coverage vs. scaffold quality.** Axis 2 must separate two questions: "does a scaffold exist for this entity?" (coverage) and "how structurally complete is that scaffold?" (quality). The brief's entry schema includes both: scaffold identifier (or gap marker) for coverage, and twelve-check pass count plus known failures for quality. These are distinct fields in the same entry, not a blended assessment. A surface with a scaffold that passes 5/12 checks and a surface with no scaffold at all are different kinds of incompleteness — the first needs remediation, the second needs production. The entry schema must make this visible. The pass count is a concrete integer, not a qualitative judgment — it's computed by applying the twelve checks, not by feeling out a maturity grade.

**Question 4 — Surface-to-scaffold lookup completeness.** NAMING-AND-ADDRESSING.md § 3.2 says: "the canonical scaffold is the highest-authority enduring descriptive scaffold whose scope includes that surface." The readiness matrix closes this by providing a lookup from surface identifier to scaffold identifier. For this to work, every surface the institution knows about must have a row in Axis 2 — either pointing to its scaffold or showing a gap marker. The Teleology Registry (CC90) enumerates 39 surfaces. All 39 need rows. Most will show gap markers. The six coding-harness surfaces will point to harness-taxonomy.md. The lookup is complete when: given any surface slug, querying Axis 2 returns either a scaffold identifier or an explicit gap declaration. No surface is silently uncovered.

**Question 5 — Authority-lattice hooks without premature drift.** The authority lattice (Document 9) will assign decision rights per authority level and tier. It will consult the readiness matrix for maturity-informed calibration — lower-maturity tiers may need more centralized decision authority; higher-maturity tiers can support broader delegation. The readiness matrix should leave clean hooks by: including maturity levels that the authority lattice can consume as inputs, but NOT including decision-rights assignments, delegation rules, or governance recommendations. The matrix says "Tier 5 is at OPEN SLOT maturity." The lattice says "at OPEN SLOT maturity, decision authority for Tier 5 is centralized to the operator." The boundary: the matrix assesses state; the lattice assigns authority based on state.
